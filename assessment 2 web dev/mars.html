Exploring Mars

Mars is known to be located in the fourth position from the Sun and is called the Red Planet because of the rust found on its surface. This planet is one of the most fascinating objects within the Solar System, and the well-developed theory states that Mars could have water in the past.

Several space exploration programs were sent to Mars in order to explore its geology, weather conditions, atmosphere, etc. Space rovers Curiosity and Perseverance helped in gaining some insights regarding the possible life conditions on the planet.

Furthermore, Mars plays an important role in the future explorations, being one of the main goals.

Why Mars Matters

The study of Mars facilitates comparison between our planet and others, and allows us to better understand planetary climates, the presence of water in our Solar System, and the challenges posed by human space travel.

In light of its proximity to Earth in relation to other planets, Mars is a feasible destination for future human space flights.
